Wood Species Notes

Lime (Tilia europaea) — Preferred

Light, straight-grained, easy to work. Resists splitting under impact. Preferred for shield construction across Scandinavian tradition. Quarter-sawn for radial grain orientation.

Scots Pine (Pinus sylvestris) — Alternate

Attested at Gokstad burial (c. 900 CE). Heavier than lime, more resinous. Acceptable substitute where lime unavailable. Pine shields found in Norse-Gaelic archaeological contexts.

Construction Sequence

Numbered assembly procedure — traditional methods

01

Plank Selection & Preparation

Quarter-sawn lime, radially grained. Rip 7 planks from seasoned stock. Thickness-plane to 3/8 in at center plank zone, 9/32 in at flanking planks, 1/4 in at outer edges. Center plank width: 4-3/4 in; flanking planks taper from 4-1/2 in at center to 2-3/8 in at rim edge. Allow wood to acclimatize 2 weeks in working environment before assembly.

02

Board Assembly

Butt-join planks edge-to-edge across 30 in span. No adhesive required — wet hide application will swell joints tight. Optionally: insert two hardwood dowels per joint for alignment during hide application. Rough-cut assembled board to circle using bow-saw, then finish edge with spokeshave to final 30 in diameter.

03

Boss Hole

Mark exact center of board. Bore 4-3/8 in diameter hole using brace & bit or keyhole saw, working from both faces to prevent blow-out. Sand edges smooth. Ensure hole is concentric — boss flange must overlap board by 2 in uniformly on all sides.

04

Board Conditioning

Coat both faces of the assembled board with raw linseed oil, applied liberally with cloth. Allow minimum 48 hours for oil to penetrate grain. This reduces splitting under stress and improves moisture resistance. Do not use boiled linseed oil — raw oil preserves wood flexibility.

05

Hide Application (Face)

Soak cattle hide overnight in water until pliable. Stretch wet hide over face of shield, pulling edges to back. Tack with iron tacks at 4 compass points first to establish even tension, then work around circumference. Allow to dry 72 hours under tension — hide will shrink tight, compressing joints and binding planks as a structural element. Trim flush at rim.

06

Hide Application (Back)

Repeat hide application process for back face. Same soak-stretch-dry procedure. Ensure boss hole remains clear of hide — trim neatly around hole edge. Back hide may be slightly thinner gauge than face hide.

07

Rim Rawhide

Soak rawhide strip (8 ft 2-1/2 in × 1 in × 3/16 in). Fold over rim edge with equal coverage on front and back faces. Tack with 1/4 in iron tacks at 1-9/16 in intervals while strip is still wet — approximately 60 tacks total for full circumference. Splice-join at bottom-rear of shield (least exposed to direct strikes). Allow 48 hours to dry and contract. Rim provides critical edge protection and structural binding.

08

Boss Forging

Forge wrought iron disc to low-dome profile (Hiberno-Norse / Insular type). Start with ~8 in round bar stock, upset to ~1/8 in thickness. Draw dome to 2-9/16 in height with 5-1/8 in diameter. Draw out flange to 9-1/16 in total diameter (2 in flange width). File smooth. Punch 5 rivet holes evenly spaced in flange. Test-fit to boss hole — flange must overlap board by 2 in uniformly. No cast examples known pre-1200 CE; all bosses must be hand-forged.

09

Boss Riveting

Seat boss in hole from face side. Mark flange rivet holes on board through hide. Drill or punch through board. Insert iron rivets from face side through flange, board, and back hide. Peen on back with dome washers to spread load. Check dome interior clears fist by at least 1 in — critical for combat grip. 5 rivets minimum, 6 for additional security.

10

Grip Bar

Forge or cut 11/16 in × 5/16 in iron flat bar to 11-3/4 in length. Slightly bow bar (~1/8 in arc) for ergonomic hand comfort. Wrap tightly with oil-dressed leather strip (23-5/8 in × 1-9/16 in), overlapping wraps by half width. Rivet through board at each end, peened on face side with washers. Grip bar must span entire boss interior diameter.

11

Strap Attachment

Attach iron suspension loops to grip rivet points on back face. Thread 35-1/2 in × 1-3/16 in leather carrying strap through loops. Strap should allow shoulder carry (slung across back for march) and wrist retention loop in combat. Adjust loop positions for balanced hang when carried. Oil strap thoroughly.

12

Paint & Finish

Apply red ochre (Fe₂O₃) in linseed oil binder to upper-left and lower-right quadrants of shield face. Apply woad blue to upper-right and lower-left quadrants. Allow 48 hours to dry between coats. Optionally: incise Norse-Gaelic knotwork triskele border at quadrant boundaries with heated iron stylus before painting (tooled-hide technique). Apply final thin coat of raw linseed oil over entire face as sealant.
